1. Describe one situation your first semester when you met someone different from yourself or those that you grew up with in your hometown.This happened around my second or third week here. I met this person at a class that I am in. We talked and what made her different was that she moved around a lot. Most people, including myself, from my hometown were born and raised there.
2. What is one thing that you learned from this person that you might not have known had you not met them? Why was that important? This may seem insignificant, but I found it interesting. I learned that in Virginia, if you are in the back or passenger seat, then you are not legally required to wear a seatbelt. I thought everyone was universally required to wear a seatbelt no matter wear you sit.
3. After viewing your midterm grades, what changes did you make your habits in the last half of the semester to improve them? I started going to math outreach consistently. I try to go at least once a week. I also joined a group for the students in this class that I am in. I made a daily schedule for myself with my study and free time on it.
4. If you had to do over again, what one thing would you have changed in your first semester at Coastal? Why would you like a “do-over” for this specific situation? I would change how I interacted with my roommates. Some things when I first got here did not bother me that much, but as the semester went that changed. I wish I had of addressed the thing when it first happened.
5. Did the reality of your first semester match your expectations at the beginning of the semester? Why or why not? No. I took college classes while in high school, so when I came here I thought it would be more or less the same. I learned very quickly that there is a very big difference between taking two college classes and being a full time college student in a new place.
