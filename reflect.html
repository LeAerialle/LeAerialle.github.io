What are/were you expecting when you arrived at Coastal? What were you excited about? What were you nervous about?
I expected people here to be friendly and for there to be nice weather. I was excited about learning new things here. I was nervous about being away from home. Besides sleeping over at a friends house, I have only been away from home one time for a weekend long summer camp, so this was pretty new for me.
What has been the hardest thing to get used to since you have arrives on campus?
The hardest thing I had to get used to was being away from home. Like I said, this is new for me and it some time getting used to. Like a month into my first semester I went grocery shopping and I kept calling my mom asking her things like what kind of milk do we get.
Socially, has it been difficult to make friends after arriving on campus? Why or why not?
It has been a little. The hardest thing was going up to new people and starting a conversation. I would get caught up in my head worrying about how they would respond like would they be friendly or just ignore me. I'm over that now. Everybody that I have met here have been friendly.
Academically, what would you say is the biggest difference between high school classes and college classes?
The biggest difference between college and high school classes is how much I have to study. It was easier in high school.
What has been the best moment since you have arrived on campus?
The moment on campus was move-in day. I had been thinking about that day a lot during my last year of high school, so when it finally happened I remember thinking "I'm here" and I was excited about my future here.
